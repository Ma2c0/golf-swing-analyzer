What Birdie does

Three small habits, one better swing.

Birdie doesn't try to be a launch monitor or a teaching pro. It's the notebook you'd keep if you had the patience to write down every swing.

01

Record

Set your phone behind the ball along your target line. Birdie helps you frame the shot — full body, level horizon, steady hands — then records a single, unhurried swing.

  • Down-the-line as the default angle
  • Ultra-wide lens support when your phone has one
  • Optional thumbs-up gesture to start and stop
  • Import an existing video from your library
02

Analyse

On-device pose tracking maps your body through setup, backswing, transition, impact and finish. You get a phase-by-phase reading that says what happened — not just a number.

  • Five-phase swing breakdown
  • Tempo, balance, and turn estimates
  • Estimated impact point on the face
  • One or two things to actually try next time
03

Journal

Every swing is filed away with the club, the date and a short note. Over a season you see what's drifting and what's holding. Quiet progress, without the dashboard fatigue.

  • Trend line of recent sessions
  • Per-club history
  • Re-open any past swing for context
  • Everything stays on your device

How a session goes

Four minutes, end to end.

  1. i.

    Set the camera down-the-line

    Four to five metres behind the ball, phone at waist height, target line running through the centre of the screen.

  2. ii.

    Take your stance

    Birdie ticks off the checklist when your full body is in frame and the lighting is good enough to read your pose.

  3. iii.

    Make one swing

    Tap the record button or give the camera a thumbs-up. One swing is enough — Birdie watches every frame.

  4. iv.

    Read the note

    You get a short headline, a score, the phase that needs the most work, and one thing to try next.

A quiet journal

The point is to notice.

Birdie doesn't gamify your range time. There are no streaks to protect, no medals to collect. Just a tidy record of what you hit and how it went — the kind of thing you'd flip back through on a quiet evening.

  • Headline that reads like a coach, not an algorithm
  • Trend line so you can see the season at a glance
  • Recent sessions list — tap any one to re-open the analysis

Questions, briefly

Things worth asking.

Do I need to make an account?

No. Birdie runs in your browser and stores swings on your own device. There is no sign-in, no cloud upload, no email capture.

Does my video get uploaded anywhere?

No. Pose tracking runs locally through MediaPipe. The recording and your journal stay on the phone or computer you used.

Will it replace a coach?

It is not trying to. Think of Birdie as a careful pair of eyes between lessons — the kind of feedback you wish you had every time you went to the range alone.

What phones does it work on?

Any modern iPhone or Android device with a recent browser. Phones with an ultra-wide lens get the bonus 0.5× wide framing so you don't have to stand impractically far back.

Can I analyse a video I already shot?

Yes. Tap Import video on the record screen, pick a clip from your library, and Birdie runs the same analysis on it.
